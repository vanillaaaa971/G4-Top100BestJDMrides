Honda Beat

The Honda Beat, made from 1991 to 1996, is a compact convertible kei car cherished for its fun, open-air driving and agile handling. With a rear mid-engine and rear-wheel-drive setup, it was one of Soichiro Honda's last projects, with approximately 33,600 units produced. Designed by Pininfarina, it took advantage of Japan's tax-friendly kei car category.
