Nissan Pulsar GTI-R

The Pulsar GTI-R, also known as the Sunny GTI-R, earned its reputation as a pocket rocket hatchback with its turbocharged engine and nimble handling. Introduced in 1990 as the high-performance model of the fourth-generation Pulsar N14, it was designed to compete in the World Rally Championship under "Group A" regulations. Powered by a 2000cc turbo engine producing 230 horsepower, it boasted a sophisticated 4WD system controlled by the "ATTESA" system.
