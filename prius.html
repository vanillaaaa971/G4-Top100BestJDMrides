Toyota Prius (first generation)

The Toyota Prius is a compact family car known for its hybrid technology, blending an internal combustion engine with an electric motor. Originally a sedan, it transitioned to a five-door liftback in 2003. Launched in Japan in 1997 and globally in 2000, it marked Toyota's venture into mass-produced hybrid vehicles. The Prius lineup expanded to include models like the Prius v and Prius c. With over 6.1 million units sold globally since 2017, it's one of Toyota's most successful models, particularly in Japan and the United States.
