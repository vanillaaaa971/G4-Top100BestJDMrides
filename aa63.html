Toyota Carina AA63

The Toyota Carina is a car produced by Toyota from 1970 to 2001. Initially designed as a sedan version of the Celica, it later shifted to the Corona platform while maintaining its sporty image. Sold exclusively in Japan at Toyota Store dealerships, it targeted the youth market. It was replaced by the Allion in Japan and the Avensis in Europe.
